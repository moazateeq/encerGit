What is Cancer Related Fatigue? (CRF)

CRF is a common and distressing side-effect of oncologic treatments, defined as a persistent and overwhelming sense of fatigue, tiredness, weakness, memory and concentration difficulties, reduced physical and mental stamina, increased irritability, and decreased ability to perform usual activities.

These symptoms can have a significant impact on a patient’s emotional and mental well-being, social interactions, and ability to work or participate in daily life. CRF impacts over 70% of all active cancer patients, with some suffering from it for long periods after their recovery.
